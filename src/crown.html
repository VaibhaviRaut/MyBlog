Crown


There once was a kingdom
Called life
There lived a Queen
She sat on her decorated throne
And wore a crown bearing a single stone
There were varieties of visits
Some left lessons
Some left memories
As their gifts
Among them came disguised foes
Who, conspired to exploit the Queen's
Handicap, sole
They offered mighty words and flashy colored stones
To attach to her simple crown
Her simple soul
Little did she know
It was the beginning of her downfall
Variety came thereon
Both friend and foes
But the Queen was unaware of the slightest weight
Gradually burdening her crown with false pride
And vain
Under the false mighty praises they bestowed
“You are the most magnificent of all
With that priceless crown,
You possess powers superior to all,
You are the Queen most beautiful.”
They said
Her eyes veiled with the high tides of
Misty gays
Her ears deaf to all her
Pure Counsels
Her brain infected with
Ego
And thus began the reign of fire
Severing all the bonds of friendships
Instead of desire
Demanding golden eggs like Midas
To add to her already heavy crown
And then the time came
Her neck bent down
With the weight that it gained
Bearing her vulnerability with
Knives pointed at her nape
By the hands of those very mouths that showered
Praise
Now spouting their long plotted
Plan
That’s how the Queen lost all that she had
Alas,
Times are cruel not a fairytale
Now long after
In a village nearby
Lived the wise old Counsel with children
All ears to his tale
About the kingdom
Called life
Which he ended tragically
Yet with a smile
As he taught the morals of abandoning
Greed,
Ego and pride
